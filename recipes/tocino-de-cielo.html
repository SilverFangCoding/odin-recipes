Spanish Tocino de cielo


Description:

This traditional dessert dates back to the 1300s in Jerez, where eggs whites were used to clarify wine, so the leftovers yolks were given to the local nuns.

With them they created this caramel dessert, and "Tocino de cielo" actually translates as "Heave's bacon" referring to its religious origins.


How to make the traditional "Tocino de cielo":

Ingredients:

  • 700g of Sugar
  • 250ml of Water
  • 15 Egg yolks

And that's all we need!, without further ado let's get into the actual preparing!

Steps:

  1. Set a steam oven to 100Â°
  2. Add 100g of sugar and 100ml of water to a small pan over a medium heat. Allow to caramelise until dark golden brown, then set aside.
  3. In a separate pan, boil the remainder of the sugar and water together for 8 minutes to make a syrup. Leave to cool.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks and gradually add in the cooled syrup. Keep whisking until fully combined in a smooth and creamy mixture, trying to not let it foam.
  5. Pour the mixture into a rectangular baking tin with deep sides. Pour over a little of the caramel sauce, reserving most of it to serve. Steam for 35 minutes then allow to cool and set.
  6. Once the Tocino de Cielo is set, turn out onto a large board or plate.
  7. Grab a spoon and enjoy!
